5. "We are seeking funding to support..."

We are seeking funding to support the research and development of a patent-protected AR clinical documentation platform with an integrated health equity intelligence engine — a system that does not yet exist in any form on the market. Specifically:

1. Clinical NLP R&D: Developing and validating noise-robust natural language processing for real-time clinical transcription in hospital environments (operating rooms, emergency departments, multi-speaker settings). Existing commercial NLP systems are trained on clean audio; clinical environments present unsolved acoustic challenges including equipment alarms, overhead pages, and simultaneous conversations. The research question is whether acceptable transcription accuracy (>95% clinical term accuracy) can be achieved in these conditions on an AR wearable form factor.

2. Health Equity Algorithm R&D: Designing, building, and validating a novel AI engine that cross-references patient demographics against evidence-based clinical disparity data to generate real-time health equity alerts. No existing EHR or clinical documentation system includes any health equity capability. Research areas include:

- Pulse oximetry bias detection algorithms for patients with darker skin tones (documented 3.56% overestimation in Black patients per Sjoding et al., NEJM 2020)

- Maternal mortality risk scoring incorporating race-adjusted factors (3-4x disparity for Black women per CDC data)

- Implicit bias detection in clinical documentation patterns

- Culturally-adapted care protocol suggestions based on patient demographics and SDOH data

- The fundamental research question: Can an AI system integrated into the clinical documentation workflow measurably reduce health equity gaps without adding cognitive burden to physicians?

3. AR Display and Human Factors R&D: Researching optimal AR heads-up display design for extended clinical use — information density, readability under variable lighting (exam room vs. OR vs. ER), cognitive load during patient interaction, and ergonomic factors affecting physician adoption. No published research exists on AR HUD design specifically for clinical documentation workflows.

4. FHIR Interoperability R&D: Developing real-time bidirectional EHR integration via FHIR R4 on a wearable device — a technical challenge not yet solved by any commercial product. Research includes latency optimization for voice-triggered EHR queries, secure PHI transmission from wearable devices, and cross-EHR compatibility (Epic, Cerner/Oracle, athenahealth, etc.).

5. Clinical Validation Study Design: Developing the methodology and pilot infrastructure to test whether AR-assisted documentation with health equity alerts produces measurable improvements in: documentation time, coding accuracy, health equity metric compliance, and physician satisfaction compared to existing ambient-only AI scribes.

19. Problem Solved

Physicians spend 2+ hours daily on clinical documentation — typing notes, clicking through EHR screens, and coding visits — instead of treating patients. This is the #1 driver of physician burnout, which causes 1 in 5 doctors to leave medicine. The documentation burden also creates downstream problems: coding errors that leak an estimated 10% of practice revenue, missed quality metrics that affect value-based care bonuses, and critical health equity gaps.

The health equity gap is the most urgent unsolved problem. No existing EHR, AI scribe, or clinical documentation tool includes any capability to detect or mitigate health disparities during the documentation process. Published research documents significant disparities:

  • Pulse oximeters overestimate oxygen levels by 3.56% in Black patients (Sjoding et al., NEJM 2020), leading to delayed treatment
  • Black women face 3-4x higher maternal mortality rates (CDC), partly due to documentation and clinical decision-making bias
  • Implicit bias in clinical documentation correlates with disparate treatment outcomes (FitzGerald & Hurst, BMC Medical Ethics 2017)
  • CMS and the Joint Commission are mandating health equity reporting, but clinicians have no tools to support compliance in real time

MDx Vision proposes to solve both problems simultaneously: an AR smart glasses platform that eliminates the keyboard (addressing burnout) while embedding an AI-driven health equity engine that surfaces disparity alerts in real time during the patient encounter (addressing equity). This dual innovation — AR form factor + health equity intelligence — represents a novel R&D challenge that no existing product or research program has attempted.

20. Differentiated Technology

The proposed system integrates multiple novel technology components requiring R&D:

  • Clinical NLP for Noisy Environments (R&D required): Real-time speech-to-text optimized for hospital acoustic conditions. Existing commercial solutions (AssemblyAI, Whisper, Deepgram) achieve high accuracy on clean audio but degrade significantly in clinical settings with equipment alarms, overhead pages, and multi-speaker conversations. Research needed on noise reduction (RNNoise adaptation), speaker diarization in clinical contexts, and medical terminology accuracy.
  • Health Equity AI Engine (R&D required — novel, no existing solution): An AI system that cross-references patient demographics (race, ethnicity, language, SDOH factors) against evidence-based clinical disparity data to generate real-time alerts during documentation. This requires: (a) building a validated clinical disparities knowledge base, (b) developing algorithms that trigger alerts without adding cognitive burden, (c) validating across diverse patient populations to avoid reinforcing bias, (d) measuring whether alerts actually change clinician behavior and improve equity outcomes. No published research or commercial product has attempted this.
  • AR HUD for Clinical Workflow (R&D required): Voice-activated heads-up display on AR glasses showing patient data, drug alerts, and health equity notifications. Research needed on information density, readability under clinical lighting conditions, cognitive load during patient interaction, and ergonomic factors for extended wear (2-3 hour clinical sessions). No published human factors research exists for AR HUDs in clinical documentation.
  • FHIR R4 Bidirectional Integration on Wearables (R&D required): Real-time read/write EHR integration from a wearable device via FHIR R4. Technical challenges include latency optimization for voice-triggered queries, secure PHI transmission from wearable hardware, and cross-vendor compatibility (Epic, Cerner, athenahealth implement FHIR differently).
  • Ambient Clinical Intelligence with Multi-Intent Parsing (R&D required): AI that listens to natural physician-patient conversation and simultaneously generates structured notes, detects medication mentions for drug interaction checking, identifies care gaps, and triggers health equity alerts — all without physician prompting. This multi-intent ambient processing on a constrained wearable device is an unsolved technical challenge.
