19. Problem Solved

Physicians spend 2+ hours daily on clinical documentation — typing notes, clicking through EHR screens, and coding visits — instead of treating patients. This is the #1 driver of physician burnout, which causes 1 in 5 doctors to leave medicine. The documentation burden also creates downstream problems: coding errors that leak revenue (estimated 10% revenue loss per practice), missed quality metrics that affect value-based care bonuses, and — critically — health equity gaps where implicit bias in documentation leads to disparate patient outcomes.

MDx Vision eliminates the keyboard entirely. Physicians wear AR smart glasses and speak naturally while examining patients. Our AI assistant Minerva listens, generates complete clinical notes with ICD-10/CPT codes, checks drug interactions against the patient's medication list, surfaces evidence-based clinical guidelines, and — uniquely — fires real-time health equity alerts (e.g., pulse oximeter accuracy warnings for darker skin tones, maternal mortality risk flags for Black women, cultural care preferences). Everything writes back directly to the EHR via FHIR. No screen between doctor and patient. No typing. No burnout.
